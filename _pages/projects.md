## Policing the Perimeter: Public Housing and Spatialized Enforcement
This project develops a theory of **perimeter policing** to explain how misdemeanor and marijuana enforcement remains spatially concentrated around public housing developments even after legal reforms.

**Methods:** GIS, spatial Durbin models, multilevel models, quasi-experimental designs  
**Status:** Manuscript in progress / under review

---

## Clean Slate Act: Record Sealing and Legal Inequality in New York
This project examines implementation and anticipated effects of automatic record sealing, focusing on eligible, soon-to-be-eligible, and non-eligible groups.

**Methods:** administrative data analysis, implementation research, mixed methods  
**Status:** Planning + design phase

---

## Immigration Courts, Policing, and Neighborhood Context
This project links local misdemeanor enforcement and neighborhood racial composition to immigration court outcomes and deportation risk.

**Methods:** linked administrative data, spatial analysis, policy analysis  
**Status:** Working paper

---

## Governing the Migrant Crisis in New York City
Using interviews, surveys, and policy analysis, this project examines emergency governance, shelter systems, and migrant experiences in NYC.

**Methods:** mixed methods, interviews, survey design, policy analysis  
**Status:** Ongoing

---

## Genealogy of Broken Windows Policing
A historical and discursive analysis tracing how Broken Windows achieved institutional durability through rhetorical flexibility and bureaucratic embedding.

**Methods:** historical institutionalism, critical discourse analysis  
**Status:** Manuscript in progress
